Ok, it's me for the last time. I'll introduce two more concepts before I don't have to hold your hand. They are called 'combinations' (un/pw) and 'forking'.

 

Combinations: some answers have multiple parts. (Like other riddle games, they are called 'username/password' levels) In EscapeHK, you will ALWAYS be prompted to enter slashes between them, like this: john/coltrane.html (example) Basically, this means that if you aren't told to put a slash, you shouldn't put a slash. And if you are told things about un/pw's or slashes, then you know what to do.

 

Forking: Sometimes there are multiple correct solutions to a level, and they lead you to two different levels. That is a 'fork'. This is not the same thing a combination, since you only need one fork to continue, technically.

Although it is wise to explore all forks, so you can get as many hints as possible.

Most of the time, you can solve a level without knowing a fork existed, or two different levels could be solved and lead to the same level and you wouldn't know.

Luckily for you, Arianna (or A) folder does not have any forks. :)
